Size of spatial memories is relative to size of us!


Have you ever experienced a mismatch in size of a place and the size of that place in your imagination when you go to a place that you have been there only when you were a little kid? My hypothesis is that we form spatial memories relative to our size. That's is to say, size of a place in our perception is relatively bigger when we are a kid as compared to when we are adult. Thus when we form a memory in childhood and go to that place in adulthood which our size doubled or maybe tripled, that place looks smaller than our imagination!
