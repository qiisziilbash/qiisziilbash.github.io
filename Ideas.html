Size of spatial memories is relative to size of us!


Have you ever experienced a mismatch in the size of a place and the size of that place in your imagination when you go to a place that you have been there only when you were a little kid? My hypothesis is that we form spatial memories relative to our size. That's to say, the size of a place in our perception is relatively bigger when we are a kid as compared to when we are an adult. Thus when we form a memory in childhood and go to that place in adulthood which our size doubled or maybe tripled, that place looks smaller than our imagination!

We all share same principal components of personality!


Our personalities are a function of atomic personality components that are common among all humans. The only difference among individuals is the "function" which I believe is the product of genetics,  epigenetics, and the environment that we live in. This idea makes more sense in deeper levels of consciousness since we are aware of these components' voice. Our actions are just the integration (function) of these component's voices. 
These components can be something like greed, honesty, arrogance, humility and so on.
